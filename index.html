Amy Winehouse

September 14, 1983 - July 23, 2011

Amy Jade Winehouse was a British singer-songwriter who skyrocketed to fame as a result of the critically acclaimed album, Back to Black (2006). The album yielded 5 Grammy awards for the singer, but her tumultuous love life, erratic behaviour, and substance abuse problems stalled her recording career and made her a consistent target of tabloid journalism. Winehouse unfortunately passed in 2011 at the young age of 27, solidifying her place in the infamous "27 Club."
